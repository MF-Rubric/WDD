Overview of Course

Psychology is the scientific study of behaviour and mental processes. The study of Psychology encompasses the biological influences, social pressures, and environmental factors that affect how people think, act, and feel. The BA (Honours) in Psychology is a three-year, level 8 course that aims to help students develop a psychologist's mindset, which is achieved by encouraging students to create and analyse factual evidence while considering the five theoretical perspectives of psychology.

Perspective Subject Matter
Behavioural How behaviour is changed under certain conditions
Biological The effects of genetics, chemistry and hormones on behaviour and cognition
Cognitive Mental processes such as attention, creativity, and reasoning
Psychodynamic The influences of unconscious motivations on behaviour and cognition
Social Social interactions

In the 1st year of the course, students will be required to take on two additional minor subjects, and on completion of the 1st year, students will be required to choose one of the two selected minor subjects to study for the rest of the course. The minor subjects are broken down into three groups students can only take one subject from each group.
